## 1. Every Cell Is Three

TIG's founding premise is "every one is three" — every entity simultaneously exists at three scales: micro (internal components), self (the entity), and macro (the environment). Each scale occupies one of three phases: Being (□), Doing (▶), or Becoming (○). This produces 3³ = 27 possible states.

A single human cell demonstrates this exactly:

**Micro (internal components):**
- Organelles maintain structure (Being)
- Metabolic pathways process energy (Doing)
- DNA repair and protein synthesis create new structure (Becoming)

**Self (the cell):**
- Membrane integrity and identity maintenance (Being)
- Signal transduction and response execution (Doing)
- Growth, division preparation, differentiation (Becoming)

**Macro (tissue/organism context):**
- Position in tissue architecture (Being)
- Communication with neighboring cells (Doing)
- Contribution to tissue remodeling and adaptation (Becoming)

The cell is always in one of 27 states depending on the combination of phases across scales. A cell in G1 phase with active signaling in a stable tissue occupies a different state than a dividing cell responding to damage signals in an inflamed tissue.

**Implementation insight:** Current cell biology measures individual parameters (gene expression, protein levels, metabolic rates). TIG suggests a composite state descriptor — the triplet code — could capture cellular status more efficiently. A cell's state as [□▶○] (Being internally, Doing at self-level, Becoming in tissue context) carries more diagnostic information than any single measurement.
